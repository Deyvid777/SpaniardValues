SpaniardValues

What is SpaniardValues?

SpaniardValues is a political test created by Deyvid#8195 which is based on Spaniard politics. You will be presented by a statement, and then you will answer with your opinion on the statement, from Strongly Agree to Strongly Disagree, with each answer slightly affecting your scores. At the end of the quiz, your answers will be compared to the maximum possible for each value, thus giving you a percentage. Answer honestly!

There are questions in the test.

What are the six axes?

Currently the test has six axes, each of which has two opposite values which are:

Capitalism vs. Communism
Those with a higher Capitalism score tend to favor Private Property, Free Trade, Competition, Division of Labor, and Social Cooperation. Those with a higher communism score tend to favor public ownership, wealth distribution, nationalization and centralization of banking, economic equality, and That workers own the means of production.

Conservatism vs. Progressivism
Those with a higher Conservatism score support the preservation of traditions, The defense of the family as the main pillar of ancient society and institutions. Those with a higher Progressivism score tend to support Social Reform, Abolition of Traditions, and Social Constructivism.

Nationalism vs. Globalism
Those with the highest scores in Nationalism tend to support the idea of Nation, Protection of national symbols, Protection of the culture and identity of the country and Defense of national sovereignty. Those with a higher Globalism score tend to support the idea of A United World, Protection of international symbols, cooperation and the integration of many cultures and Diplomacy.

Republicanism vs. Monarchism
Those with the highest scores in Republicanism support the idea of Spain becoming a republic again and the abolition of the monarchy. Those with a higher Monarchism score support the idea that Spain continues to have the current monarchy against a republic.

Unionism vs. Separatism
Those with the highest scores in Unionism support the idea that Spain should be a united country against regional separatisms. Those with a higher Separatism score support the idea of balkanize Spain by supporting separatist movements.

What is the "closest match" in the results?

Like 8 values, this test tries to match it with more characteristic political ideologies of Spain. All of this is currently W.I.P.
